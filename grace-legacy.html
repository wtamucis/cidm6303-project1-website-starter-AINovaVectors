“Her heart overflowed beyond earth.”

We were overjoyed to learn we were expecting, and from the very beginning we followed every step with our doctor...At our first sonogram, the healthcare team discovered growth near her skull. A genetics test confirmed that our baby had Turner’s Syndrome. Devastated by the news, we clung to hope, even after reading that 98% of pregnancies with Turner’s Syndrome end in miscarriage. We believed she could be the 2% who would make it. For months we lived in fear and hope, holding on to every heartbeat, every sign of her energy and determination.

At 22 weeks, we traveled to another city to see a maternal-fetal medicine specialist. The week before, during a sonogram with our doctor, we had heard her heartbeat—strong and full of life. She was fighting to be here with us. But at the specialist’s office, the technician left the room suddenly, and we knew. We were met with doctors and nurses who tried to console us, but the weight of that moment was unbearable. Our doctor called immediately afterward and asked us to come in the next morning. That night was filled with tears and no sleep. The next day, surrounded by a tense room of medical staff, we delivered our little girl. We never heard her cry, but she was so beautiful to us—our baby, our best friend, our Grace. Even as we prepared for delivery, we were asked about funeral services and epidurals. Nothing can prepare you for those conversations. You simply walk through them because there is no other choice.

The days that followed were filled with grief and emptiness. We sought group therapy, journaling, and tried to find fragments of normalcy. It has been months since losing Grace, and not a day passes without thinking of her. We remind ourselves that her love was too pure, too good for this earth. In her short time, she gave us a lifetime of love. We have found comfort in connecting with other parents who have experienced similar losses, learning to support one another even though no two grief journeys are the same. Grace’s legacy lives on in the strength she gave us, in the community we’ve found, and in the love that will never fade.
