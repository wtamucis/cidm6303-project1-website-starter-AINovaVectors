“Love endures, even through loss.”

No words can truly ease the pain of losing a child. It’s a grief that reshapes everything, and the healing process is neither linear nor predictable. You may feel lost, angry, numb, or desperate for answers—and every one of those emotions is valid. There is no timeline for healing, no “right” way to grieve. What you’re feeling is not only normal—it’s a reflection of deep love

As you search for meaning and try to find a new rhythm, know that you don’t have to do it alone. Staying connected with friends and family can offer gentle reminders that you are still held. Group therapy and counseling services can provide a safe space to speak freely, cry openly, and be heard without judgment. Journaling may help you process the waves of emotion—whether it’s a single word, a memory, or a prayer.

This page is here to walk beside you. Whether you’re seeking resources, community, or simply a quiet place to breathe, you are welcome here. Grace’s legacy lives on in every step you take toward healing. You are not alone, and you are never forgotten.

Explore Support Groups

Grief is not meant to be carried alone. Whether you’re ready to speak or simply need a quiet place to listen, support groups offer connection, understanding, and a safe space to begin healing.

  • The Hope & Healing Place offers free grief support groups for families, including programs for infant and child loss. Their sessions use art, storytelling, and shared experience to help families process grief together. Visit hopeandhealingplace.org or call (806) 371-8998 to learn more.
  • The Compassionate Friends provides online communities and local chapters for families grieving the loss of a child at any age. Their forums and virtual groups offer friendship, understanding, and hope. Explore at compassionatefriends.org.
  • griefHaven offers specialized virtual support groups for different types of child loss, including miscarriage, stillbirth, and sudden death. Their facilitators are often bereaved parents themselves. Learn more at griefhaven.org.
