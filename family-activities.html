“Together we plant healing love.”

Grief touches every member of a family, including the youngest. While toddlers may not fully understand loss, they feel the emotions around them. Families can find comfort in creating rituals and activities that honor their loved one while nurturing resilience and connection. These shared practices remind children that love continues to grow, even in difficult times.

Shared Comfort Rituals

  • Butterfly Release – A symbolic way to honor a loved one’s spirit and invite beauty into the moment.
  • Shadow Bow Keepsake – Collect photos, footprints, or small tokens to create a tangible memory box.
  • Family Garden – Plant flowers or a tree together, teaching children that love continues to grow.

Future Ideas for Families

  • Conversation Guides – A symbolic way to honor a loved one’s spirit and invite beauty into the moment.
  • Journaling & Art Projects – Encourage older children to express feelings through words, drawings, or creative play.
  • Counseling & Group Therapy –– Provide safe, supportive environments for children to share grief and learn coping skills.
